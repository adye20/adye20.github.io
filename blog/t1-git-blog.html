Version Control, Tracking Changes, and Github

Introduction of Concepts

December 12th, 2014

What are the benefits of version control? Version control has many benefits such as managing the control of files being passed from one person to another, maintaining a detailed historical log, and file recovery. Now you may be asking yourself, what benefit is it to me if I’m careful with controlling my files? From personal experience, it provides huge benefit especially from uncontrollable accidents. I was working on a cross-functional engineering team budget for satellite mass, power, and thermal margins, and as an electrical engineer I am responsible for the power budget. The thermal engineer and I were collaborating on his computer and made all the appropriate calculations for the proposed design. As we finished up our budget, I got called into another frontend proposal meeting. Two hours later, the thermal engineer called me to tell me that there was some last minute design changes which the mechanical engineer, who was in charge of the mass properties, and him were working on to close the budgets. Normally this wouldn’t be a problem as last minute designs come in all the time except I didn’t save a version of the budget on my computer. Big mistake! Since they were working to the updated design, this changed all of the power calculations and I had no way to revert back to see what was changed and what was added.
